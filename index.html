Get To Know Me

Full Name & Nickname:

Hello! My name is Yuan Alexandrei A. Serafico. My name is already short, but some people used to call me Yuyu as a term of endearment. In the late 90's there was a sitcom called G-mik, where John Pratts played the character Yuan. Somehow my mother took a liking to that name and gave it to me, and Alexandrei was made up to match my older sister's second name, Alexandra. Thus, began the cycle of confusing people due to the mismatch of my name and sex.

Age & Birthdate:

On June 6, 2002, at 8:41 a.m., in the uppity hospital Makati Medical Center, a baby girl of 2,892 grams with a dimple on each cheek was born. She is now 19 years, 5 months, and 29 days old.

High School:

For the first four years of my secondary education I suffered and thrived in Makati Science High School. Wanting to pursue a creative track, I transferred to Asia Pacific College (APC) to study Multimedia Arts and finished Senior High School there. Both are located in Makati City.

Course & Why:

Funny enough, for college I am studying Information Technology (I.T.), majoring in Mobile and Internet Technologies. Everyone's dream for me was to study in some big university, and I also thought it was my dream. However, I knew the pressure would kill me and I wanted to be kind to myself (especially after barely getting out of a science high school alive). So, I decided to stay in APC. And since its specialty is I.T., and I started college during the pandemic, I thought maybe it's best to study this (Ha!). Now on my second year, I stuck to this course even though I still feel lost, since this is not my #1 passion.

Scholarship:

This student is a Makati Scholar (Thanks, Abby!). It is not a percent type, but they give out 40,000 pesos to each scholar every year through GCash. Very convenient. I applied for the SM Foundation scholarship and passed the test, but alas, after the virtual home visit, they decided I wasn't poor enough. We can afford a comfortable life, but we can't afford college tuition.

I.T. Experience:

I'm embarrassed to say, but my only I.T. experiences are from APC. In senior high we made a website, but I've forgotten everything about it. Last term we made a couple of games and I was assigned to be the game designer, UI designer, sound engineer, game scriptwriter, and documentation team. I think I can program if I wanted to and self-studied, but most of my classmates are already experienced in making games, so I let them take the reins. Sometimes I beat myself up about not being one of the programmers, but I realize that each member of the team bring their own expertise (and mine is story writing).

Hobbies & Interests:

Since forever I have loved stories. That's why I love reading books and graphic novels as well as watching films and series. Another thing so dear in my heart is theatre. The music, dance, acting, sets, costumes, the audience… everything about it is divine! In all honesty, that is my true dream, but it's expensive as heck to pursue it. I started to get into musicals in ninth grade, and around senior high kind of forgot about it, but in the past couple of months I have been once again obsessed. I have been a member of Teatré Phileo, APC's theatre guild, since 2018, and I am currently the External Vice President. Once we have face-to-face classes, I'll pounce on the opportunity to write and direct a play!

Life Goals:

This may sound cheesy, but I really just want to be happy. For now, I do not know what will bring me that. However, to sound like my life has a direction, I'll say my goal is to use my (future) I.T. expertise in theatre and film. Perhaps in art, too.

Course Expectations & Wants:

Obvious from the course code, I expect to learn website programming in-depth. In senior high, for two consecutive terms we had the same teacher who just made us copy codes from a printed copy. Suffice to say that I did not learn anything. I do have high hopes for this class since we will utilize Khan Academy, and I was delighted with the experience of using it. Maybe for my major (MI), website coding for mobile would be helpful.
